Police Body Cameras: The Best Thinking So Far

For the past year and a half, Americans have been very interested in police body cameras - most specifically, why more cops don't have them. As I go around the country having conversations with police agencies and citizens, they're often as shocked to hear that, in 2013, while 81% of police officers have TASERs, only about a third (32%) have body camerasBJS NCJ 248767, Dr. Brian A. Reaves, July 7, 2015. While you'd expect that number to have increased by this year, it's an interesting data-point that according to StreetCred Police Killings in Context data, video was only available in 26% of cases in which a police officer killed an unarmed civilian. And while most people know that, in two 2015 cases, witness video was crucial to show officers were not truthful with investigators, most don't know that it fully exonerated the officers in two other 2015 cases.

What is most surprising is that, in all four of these cases, video was photographed by bystanders or surveillance cameras, not police video.
